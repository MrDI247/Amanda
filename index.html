Charles Ranlett Flint

(1850-1934)

“Ambition and thirst for power have a part but greed and greed alone is the reason for a man wanting to swell his ward.”

Contributions:

He was an American businessman and best known as the founder of the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company that became IBM.

Timeline:

1850- Charles Ranlett Flint was born on January 24,1850 in Thomas, Maine. He was an only child until later years. The family had moved from Maine to New York where his father ran mercantile firm Chapman and Flint; it was founded in 1837.

1868- He graduated from Brooklyn Polytechnic Institute in Brooklyn (that is now New York University - Tandon School of Engineering)

1871- He entered the shipping business as a partner in Gilchrest, Flint and Co in 1871. After a transaction with the ownership of the companies, he entered W.R. Grace which was an American chemical business based in Columbia, Maryland.

1876 to 1879- Charles Flint served as the Chilean consul at New York City and served as consul general to the United States for Nicaragua and Costa Rica.

1892- He consolidated several companies to form U.S. Rubber., which is an American manufacturer of tires and other synthetic rubber-related products. As well as variety of items for military use

1893- He outfitted a fleet of naval ships for the Brazilian Republic in 1893. During the First Sino-Japanese War,he purchased the Esmeralda from the Chilean Navy and delivered it to Japan via Ecuador.

1899- He did the same thing in 1899 with Adams Chewing Gum, Chiclets, Dentyne, and Beemans to form American Chicle. He was also the driving force behind the establishment of American Woolen in 1899.

1911- He merged four companies to form the Computing-Tabulating-Recording Company: The Tabulating Machine Company, International Time Recording Company, Computing Scale Company of America, and Bundy Manufacturing Company.

1930- Charles Flint served on IBM's board of directors until his retirement in 1930.

1934- He died in Washington, D.C. on February 26, 1934.
